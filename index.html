Executive Summary

The world is about to run very short of copper.

Copper is no longer just a commodity. It is the central nervous system of the 21st-century economy — every EV battery, every solar panel, every AI data center, every defense radar system runs on it.

S&P Global projects a 50% surge in copper demand by 2040 — from 28 million to 42 million metric tons annually — alongside a 10 million MT cumulative supply deficit that recycling and efficiency gains simply cannot close. That is not a cycle. That is a structural shift.

Against this backdrop, four pure-play copper miners stand out: Freeport-McMoRan (FCX), the undisputed industry giant; Southern Copper (SCCO), the margin aristocrat; Hudbay Minerals (HBM), the fastest-growing underdog; and Teck Resources (TECK), the pivot play remaking itself into a copper-focused major.

The key finding: SCCO is almost certainly overvalued at 30x+ earnings with a 60% analyst sell consensus. HBM is almost certainly undervalued at 13x earnings with 100% analyst buys and a balance sheet transformation most investors have not yet repriced.

Macro Thesis

Supply is structurally broken

S&P Global’s January 2026 study projects demand rising from 28 million MT to 42 million MT by 2040, creating a 10 million MT supply shortfall. The ICSG identifies a 150,000 MT deficit for 2026 specifically — the first structural shortage since 2009.

J.P. Morgan is forecasting a 330,000 MT refined copper deficit in 2026 with a price target of $12,500/mt. Spot copper has already touched $14,500/mt intraday, currently trading above $13,000/mt.

Why can’t supply catch up? Mine permitting takes 15–20 years. Chile — the world’s largest copper producer — has 13 projects worth $14.8 billion targeting 2026, adding roughly 500,000 MT — a fraction of the projected deficit. Meanwhile, grades are declining, costs are rising, and geopolitical friction is intensifying.

Company Profiles

The Contenders

Freeport-McMoRan (FCX) — The Industry Heavyweight

The largest publicly traded pure-play copper producer, with a $78.6B market cap and the crown jewel: Grasberg in Indonesia. Revenue of $25.7B in FY2025, $2.2B net income, $1.1B FCF.

The Grasberg restart in Q2 2026 is the key catalyst. Analysts project $29.3B in FY2026 revenue and $3.65B in FCF — FCF essentially triples. The forward P/E compresses toward 12.4x.

The concern: at 36x trailing earnings, Simply Wall St flags FCX as ~39.6% overvalued. The stock is down ~20% from its February 2026 high of $69.75. $8.1B net debt is serviceable but not light.

Southern Copper (SCCO) — The Margin Machine

The most profitable copper miner on earth. 60% gross margin, 52% operating margin, 32% net margin. Revenue of $13.3B, $4.3B net income, ROIC of 26.5%.

The problem is the price tag. At $159.28 with $131.6B market cap, SCCO trades at 30.6x trailing earnings. With 60% of analysts rated bearish and average PT of $136.25 — 14.5% below the current price — the risk/reward tilts unfavorably.

Grupo Mexico’s 89% control creates governance concerns. SCCO is a great business but a challenged investment at current prices.

Hudbay Minerals (HBM) — The Dark Horse

The one hiding in plain sight. Revenue grew from $1.57B in FY2023 to $2.63B in FY2025 — a 68% increase. Net income swung from a $57M loss to $516M. FCF went from $42M to $478M.

Net debt fell from $1.3B to just $500M. D/E ratio of 0.24 — the healthiest of the four. Yet the stock trades at 13.2x trailing earnings, a 2x discount to FCX.

100% analyst buy consensus. Average PT of $26.00 against $19.05 — 36% implied upside. High target of $32.50 implies 71%. The most asymmetric opportunity in the group.

Teck Resources (TECK) — The Pivot Play

Mid-transformation after divesting steelmaking coal. Quebrada Blanca Phase 2 (QB2) in Chile is the cornerstone. $23.3B market cap, $11.5B FY2025 revenue.

The headline number that looks worst: negative $645M in FCF, driven by $4.0B in capex. This is not distress — it’s investment. $2.1B cash on hand.

When QB2 reaches full capacity (expected 2026–2027), copper output roughly doubles. Analyst consensus buy, average PT $53.33, high target $67.

Bull Case

Why copper could be the trade of the decade

The deficit is real and widening. The ICSG’s 150,000 MT shortfall for 2026 is the opening bid. S&P Global’s 10 million MT cumulative deficit through 2040 is not a fringe view.

AI/data center demand is the new wildcard. 475,000 tonnes demanded by AI data centers in 2026 — larger than the world’s sixth-largest copper mine, added in a single year. BHP forecasts 6x growth by 2050.

Electrification is structural, not cyclical. EVs use 60–90kg of copper per vehicle. Grid-scale battery storage, offshore wind, and solar all require copper-intensive components.

FCX’s Grasberg restart is a near-term earnings catalyst that compresses forward P/E toward 12x. HBM is the clearest mispricing at 13.2x earnings with 100% analyst buy coverage and rapid deleveraging.

Bear Case

What could go wrong

China remains the wild card. China consumes ~50% of global copper. A hard landing could swing demand by 2–3 million MT in a single year. No amount of AI demand offsets that shock.

$14,500/mt is not a guaranteed floor. Prices at those levels historically trigger demand destruction and accelerated supply response. A reversion to $9,000–10,000/mt would dramatically compress earnings.

SCCO is priced for perfection. 60% of analysts rate it a sell. The $131.6B market cap is larger than FCX despite less than half the revenue.

Recession risk is real. Copper has a well-documented beta to global industrial production. In the 2015–16 slowdown, copper fell from $3.50/lb to $2.00/lb. The secular story is intact, but cyclical corrections can be severe.

Widest range of outcomes. If QB2 delivers, FCF flips from negative to potentially $2B+ annually, and the stock re-rates. Analyst high target of $67 (39% upside). A bet worth making in a small position.

Risks & Catalysts

What to watch

Catalysts

  • Grasberg restart (FCX) — Q2 2026 production triggers step-change in earnings

  • Copper sustained above $12,500/mt — dramatically expands FCF across all names
  • AI infrastructure capex acceleration — incremental copper demand supports deficit thesis

Risks

  • China demand shock — hard landing could reduce copper demand by 2–3M MT
  • Copper price correction from $14,500/mt highs — speculative unwind risk
  • Chile supply response overshoots — 13 projects could narrow near-term deficit
  • Geopolitical risk in key jurisdictions — Indonesia, Peru, Chile
